Innovating Drone Technology, Wherever We Are

Founded in 2024, Athabasca University Autonomous Aerial Systems (AUAAS) is the first fully remote student-led UAS design team in Canada. United by our passion for aerospace innovation, we collaborate across provinces to design, build, and compete with cutting-edge unmanned aerial systems.

Driven by Innovation, Powered by Collaboration

Our mission is to advance drone technology while creating opportunities for students to gain hands-on experience in aerospace systems. We aim to prove that distance is no barrier to teamwork, engineering excellence, or innovation.

Vision

To set the standard for remote engineering collaboration in Canada and inspire the next generation of autonomous aerial systems innovators.

Competing on the National Stage

AUAAS participates in major student UAS competitions, including the Aerial Evolution Association of Canada (AEAC) Student Competition. These events test our skills in engineering, autonomy, and teamwork—pushing boundaries while representing Athabasca University.

  • 2025: AEAC—Phase 1 proposal submitted; foundational lessons learned.
  • 2026: Target—field a complete system and compete end-to-end.
